The game

More than cards.
A mind game.

Powerstack is built on the foundation of a classic card game played in living rooms and kitchens for generations — but elevated with a tactical ruleset that rewards memory, deduction, and psychological pressure.

Players take turns building ascending or descending runs, switching suits through matching ranks. The goal is simple: empty your hand first. The execution is anything but.

Every turn is a question. Do you play your black jack now, or hold it for when your opponent is one card from winning? Do you stack the 2 penalty on the player in last place — or the player who's been too quiet, too comfortable, too dangerous?

Declare "I'm on cards" when you believe you can win next turn — but only if you actually can. A false declaration costs you two cards. A correct one sends a shockwave around the table.

Power cards

Six cards.
Infinite chaos.

Six ranks carry special powers that can redirect the entire course of a game. Stack them. Counter them. Time them perfectly.

A
Ace — Suit changer

Declare any suit. The next player must match it, play another Ace, or draw. Stack Aces to override the declared suit each time.

Wild
2
Two — Pick up 2

The next player draws 2 cards — unless they play another 2 to pass the penalty on. Penalties stack across players. Two 2s means 4 cards. Three means 6.

Stackable
8
Eight — Miss a turn

The next player skips their go. Play two 8s in one turn and the next two players both miss. Counter with another 8 to redirect the skip.

Skip
J♠ J♣
Black Jack — Pick up 7

The next player draws 7 cards. Stack two Black Jacks and they draw 14. The only counter is a Red Jack — which cancels the penalty entirely.

Stackable

Red Jack — Shield

Cancels an incoming Black Jack penalty completely. Your most valuable defensive card. Play it at the right moment and watch the table react.

Counter
Q
Queen — Cover card

Must be followed by a card of the same suit. Play it alone and draw a card as penalty. Stack Queens — each covers the last, until the final one needs a same-suit cover.

K
King — Reverse

Reverses the direction of play. Stack Kings to reverse multiple times. Two Kings cancel each other out. Three Kings leaves you with one net reversal. Use it to send a penalty back to its sender.

How to play

Up and running
in minutes.

01 🃏
Deal 7 cards

Each player receives 7 cards. The remaining deck sits face-down. The top card is flipped to start the discard pile.

02 🎯
Match to play

On your turn, play a card that matches the top discard by suit or rank. Can't play? Draw one card and your turn ends.

03 ⚡
Build combos

Play multiple cards in a single turn — ascending or descending runs. Switch suits by matching rank. The longer the combo, the faster you empty your hand.

04 🏆
Empty your hand

First to play all their cards wins. But you cannot finish on a power card — play it and draw one more. Declare "I'm on cards" when victory is within reach.

"The cards you hold are only half the game. The other half is making your opponents believe the wrong thing about them."

Track what's been played. Every card that hits the discard pile is information. Know what's left in the deck and you know what threats remain.

Time your power cards. A black jack played too early is wasted. Held until an opponent is one card from winning — it's devastating.

Choose your target. With four players you're managing a table, not just one opponent. Who do you hurt? Who do you let recover? Every decision has consequences two turns away.

The declaration gamble. "I'm on cards" tells everyone you're dangerous. It also paints a target on you. The bluff, the counter-bluff, the fold — Powerstack rewards those who read the room.
